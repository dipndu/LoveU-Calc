What Not To Do: Common Rookie Mistakes

I‚Äôve generated hundreds of edits with Gemini, and trust me, there is a right way and a wrong way to use a Google Gemini AI photo editing prompt. If your photos look "plastic" or fake, you are likely making one of these three mistakes:

  • Don't Ignore Lighting: This is the #1 giveaway of an AI edit. You cannot put a dark indoor selfie on a bright sunny beach background without fixing the light. Always add "Match the lighting on the subject to the new background" to your prompt.
  • Stop Being Vague: If you just say "Make me look better," Gemini will hallucinate and make your face a cartoon. You have to be specific. Ask for something like "Warm sunset glow" or "Professional studio contrast."
  • Avoid "Celebrity" Names: Don't ask to "Look like Shah Rukh Khan." Gemini's safety filters will block it instantly. Instead, describe the style like "Give me a 90s Bollywood superstar hairstyle."

Pro Tip: If Gemini refuses an edit due to "Safety Guidelines," try again after cropping the photo slightly. If it still does not work, focus your prompt on the clothing rather than the body. It works almost every time!
